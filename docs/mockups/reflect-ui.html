The premise of Reflect is that you are writing the explanation and the AI only annotates it. Three displays that make that true visually — same data underneath, only the skin changes.

Reflect · Hall-Petch & grain size

Hall-Petch: yield strength rises as grain size shrinks — $\sigma_y = \sigma_0 + k/\sqrt{d}$. Finer grains mean more grain boundaries, and boundaries block dislocation motion¹, so the metal gets stronger without losing much ductility.

When a dislocation reaches a boundary the slip plane orientation changes, so it can't simply glide across — it piles up with others behind it until the local stress concentration is big enough to trigger a source in the neighbouring grain².

The idea: your reflection reads as one continuous manuscript in serif — the artifact you're actually producing. The AI never gets a "message"; it gets a pencil note in the margin, anchored (¹ ²) to the phrase it questions, highlighted softly in the text. Answered notes fade with a ✓; the live one glows ochre. Writing continues seamlessly at the caret — no send-bubble rhythm. On narrow screens the margin folds inline (variant B, automatically). Saving stores exactly this document as the topic's content.
